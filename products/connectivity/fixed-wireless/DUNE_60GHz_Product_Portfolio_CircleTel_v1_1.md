## 2. Technology Overview

Peraso Inc. is a pioneer in mmWave wireless technology, with over 15 years of experience and more than 2 million chipsets deployed globally. The DUNE platform specifically addresses dense urban and multi-tenant connectivity challenges.

### 2.1 60GHz Spectrum Advantages

The 60GHz band offers unique characteristics that make it ideal for high-density wireless distribution:

- **Licence-free operation:** South Africa allocates 57–71 GHz for unlicensed use (6 channels at 2.16 GHz bandwidth)
- **No Wi-Fi interference:** Completely separate from congested 2.4/5/6 GHz bands
- **Spatial isolation:** Narrow beams and oxygen absorption provide natural interference mitigation
- **High bandwidth:** Up to 14 GHz of available spectrum for massive capacity

| Technology | Best Use Case | Limitation | DUNE Solution |

### 3.2 Integration with DFA Backhaul

The recommended architecture combines DFA fibre backhaul with DUNE wireless distribution, creating a cost-effective hybrid solution:

1. **DFA Lumic:** Multi-tenant dark fibre at R5 045–5 311/month provides unlimited bandwidth backhaul
2. **ECHO SP Core:** BNG/BRAS services at Teraco for subscriber management
3. **DUNE Distribution:** 60GHz wireless to up to 48 tenants per access point sector
4. **CPE + Router:** Professional Wi-Fi 6 router at each tenant for internal distribution

## 4. Target Market Segments

### 4.1 Primary Markets

#### 4.1.1 Office Parks and Business Parks

**Market Opportunity:** Gauteng alone has over 200 office parks with 10–100+ tenants each, many with inadequate or expensive connectivity options.

- Typical deployment: 2–4 DN170G access points serving 20–80 tenants
- Revenue potential: R30 000–120 000 monthly per site
- Key value proposition: Single point of contact, bundled managed services

#### 4.1.2 Multi-Dwelling Units (MDUs)

**Market Opportunity:** Residential estates, apartment complexes, and sectional title developments seeking alternatives to individual fibre connections.

- Typical deployment: 1–2 access points per building cluster
- Synergy with SafeGuard security solutions
- Body corporate partnership model

#### 4.1.3 Industrial Parks and Logistics Hubs

**Market Opportunity:** Warehouses, distribution centres, and light industrial facilities requiring reliable connectivity without construction disruption.

- Higher tolerance for functional over aesthetic installations
- IoT and operational technology integration opportunities
- Extended range with DC170P065 CPE (up to 2km)

### 4.2 Secondary Markets

- **Shopping Centres:** Tenant connectivity and public Wi-Fi backhaul
- **Healthcare Campuses:** Hospital and clinic clusters (synergy with Unjani Clinics)
- **Educational Institutions:** Campus-wide distribution from central aggregation
- **Hospitality Venues:** Conference centres, hotels, event facilities

## 7. Network Architecture Options

### 7.1 Reference Architecture: Office Park

The recommended architecture leverages CircleTel's existing partnerships and infrastructure:

**Layer 1 – Core Network (at Teraco via ECHO SP)**
- Redundant IP transit via multiple Tier 1 carriers
- BNG/BRAS for subscriber session management
- CGNAT for IPv4 address management
- NAPAfrica peering for local content

**Layer 2 – Backhaul (DFA Fibre)**
- DFA Lumic or Calypte Metro to business park aggregation point
- Bandwidth: 1–10 Gbps depending on tenant count
- Optional redundancy via secondary DFA path or MTN Tarana backup

**Layer 3 – Distribution (DUNE 60GHz)**
- DN170G-AP access points mounted at central aggregation point
- 2–3 sectors providing 240°–360° coverage
- DN170L-CPE for extended range or point-to-point backhaul between buildings

**Layer 4 – Access (Tenant CPE)**
- DC170P065 or DN170L-CPE at each tenant location
- External mount on building facade or window
- PoE connection to indoor Wi-Fi 6 router

### 7.2 Alternative: Hybrid Tarana + DUNE

For locations where DFA fibre is not available, MTN Tarana FWA can provide the primary backhaul with DUNE handling multi-tenant distribution:

1. MTN Tarana RN (100–200 Mbps) as backhaul connection
2. Aggregation switch with VLAN separation
3. DUNE DN170G-AP for tenant distribution
4. Limited to ~15–20 tenants per Tarana link
