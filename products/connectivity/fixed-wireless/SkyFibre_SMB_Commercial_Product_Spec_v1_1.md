## 1. Executive Summary

This Commercial Product Specification (CPS) defines the complete commercial, technical, and operational parameters for the SkyFibre SMB portfolio — CircleTel's primary revenue-driving product line delivering business-grade fixed wireless broadband to South African small and medium enterprises.

SkyFibre SMB leverages MTN's Wholesale Fixed Wireless Broadband (FWB) service, utilising Tarana G1 beamforming technology operating on licensed spectrum. The service delivers **asymmetrical speeds at a 4:1 download-to-upload ratio** (e.g. 100 Mbps down / 25 Mbps up), which is a network-level configuration applied by MTN across their entire FWB infrastructure. CircleTel, as a wholesale partner, inherits this profile.

The product line is unaffected by the MTN AirFibre residential channel conflict that prompted CircleTel's strategic pivot from consumer ISP to business services provider. However, a separate competitive dynamic exists with **MTN Business Uncapped 5G packages** (R449–R949 incl. VAT), which sell on the same underlying MTN network at lower headline prices but without the business SLA, static IP, managed router, and dedicated support that SkyFibre SMB includes. This channel conflict, first raised via the Delphius Midrand objection (PJ Phike), is addressed in Section 15.

The portfolio comprises three packages — Essential (50 Mbps), Professional (100 Mbps), and Premium (200 Mbps) — each offering static IP addressing, business-grade routers with cloud management, and SLA-backed uptime guarantees. The target monthly recurring revenue (MRR) for the product line is R800 000.

### 2.2 Speed Profile Clarification

## 10. Fair Usage & Acceptable Usage Policy

SkyFibre SMB is marketed as "unlimited" data. A Fair Usage Policy applies to protect network quality for all users. Business customers operate under a more generous contention model (8:1) compared to residential (35:1).

### 10.1 Business FUP Thresholds

| Package | Soft Cap | Hard Cap | Action After Soft Cap |
|---------|----------|----------|-----------------------|
| Essential 50/12.5 | 750 GB | 1.5 TB | Deprioritisation during peak (18:00–23:00) |
| Professional 100/25 | 1 TB | 2 TB | Deprioritisation during peak |
| Premium 200/50 | 1.5 TB | 3 TB | Speed management only |

### 10.2 Protected Traffic (Never Throttled)

VoIP and video conferencing (Zoom, Teams, WhatsApp calls), banking and financial services, educational platforms, work VPN connections, and email/messaging are classified as protected traffic and are never subject to FUP management.

### 10.3 Prohibited Activities

Running servers (web, mail, game), cryptocurrency mining, reselling or sharing connections, continuous streaming/broadcasting, automated bot traffic, and network attacks or scanning are prohibited under the Acceptable Usage Policy.

### 15.2 MTN Business Channel Conflict — PJ Phike / Delphius Midrand Objection

> **⚠️ STRATEGIC RISK: CHANNEL CONFLICT WITH WHOLESALE SUPPLIER**

The core objection, first raised by PJ Phike at Delphius Midrand, is that CircleTel's SkyFibre SMB products compete directly with MTN Business's own Uncapped 5G retail packages. Both services run on the same underlying MTN mobile/wireless infrastructure. MTN Business packages are priced at R449–R949 incl. VAT, while SkyFibre SMB is priced at R1 899–R4 499 excl. VAT.

This creates a perception challenge where a prospect can compare headline prices and question why they should pay a premium through CircleTel. The sales team must be equipped to articulate the value differentiation clearly.

### 15.4 Handling the Price Objection — Sales Framework

When a prospect raises the MTN Business pricing comparison, the recommended sales response follows the TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) and TCV (Total Cost of Downtime) framework:

**1. TOTAL COST OF OWNERSHIP:** MTN Business 5G at R949/month appears cheaper, but excludes the router (R3 999), static IP add-on, email hosting, cloud backup, and professional installation. SkyFibre SMB Professional at R2 899/month bundles all of this. When compared on an all-in basis, the effective gap narrows to approximately R1 200/month.

**2. COST OF DOWNTIME:** A business generating R50 000/month in online transactions loses R2 083 per business day of downtime. The SkyFibre SLA guarantees resolution within 4–8 hours with financial credits. MTN Business 5G has no service credit mechanism. One unresolved outage can wipe out a full year's perceived savings.

**3. FUP THROTTLE RISK:** MTN Business 5G Enterprise throttles to 2 Mbps after 1.5 TB. For a 15-person office streaming, using cloud apps, and running VoIP, 1.5 TB can be exhausted in 2–3 weeks. SkyFibre SMB is truly uncapped with no hard throttle.

**4. TECHNOLOGY DIFFERENCE:** SkyFibre uses dedicated Tarana G1 FWA on licensed spectrum, not shared mobile 5G. This means consistent sub-5 ms latency versus 15–30 ms, and no congestion from consumer mobile traffic.

### 15.5 Recommended Positioning Statement

> *"MTN Business 5G is excellent for backup connectivity or small offices under 5 users. But for your core business connection — where VoIP quality, guaranteed uptime, and uncapped data matter — SkyFibre SMB is the professional-grade solution. It's the difference between consumer wireless and business infrastructure."*

### 15.6 When to Recommend MTN Business 5G Instead

CircleTel should proactively recommend MTN Business 5G (via the Arlan partnership) in these scenarios: sole practitioners with < 5 users and basic email/web needs; temporary or pop-up office locations; backup/failover connectivity for fibre-primary sites; budget-constrained micro businesses. This positions CircleTel as a trusted advisor rather than a single-product vendor, and earns 30% commission on the MTN Business deal via the Arlan channel.
