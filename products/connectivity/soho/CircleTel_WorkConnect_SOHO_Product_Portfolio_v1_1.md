## 1. Executive Summary

WorkConnect™ is CircleTel's new technology-agnostic SOHO connectivity product line, designed to bridge the gap between residential broadband (HomeFibreConnect at R799–R1,999/month) and full business-grade services (SkyFibre SMB at R1,899–R6,999/month). The product targets South Africa's rapidly growing segment of freelancers, remote workers, micro-businesses (1–5 people), e-commerce operators, and content creators who need more than a residential connection but don't require enterprise SLAs.

### Strategic Rationale

The SOHO segment represents CircleTel's largest unaddressed opportunity. Key market drivers include:

- South African freelancers earned R4.8 billion on international platforms in 2024, with remote work growing 312% globally since 2020.
- Only 14% of SA households have a fixed internet connection as of Q1 2025, yet 78.9% of the population (50.8 million people) are internet users — indicating massive under-penetration of quality fixed broadband.
- Youth unemployment above 45% (Stats SA, Q2 2025) is driving self-employment and gig-economy participation.
- SA's Digital Nomad Visa (operational from March 2025) is attracting international remote workers seeking affordable, reliable connectivity.
- Competitors like Trusc already differentiate "Home Office / WFH" packages with enhanced upload speeds at R439–R1,199/month, validating demand.

**Naming Rationale:** "WorkConnect" follows CircleTel's [Descriptor]Connect naming convention (HomeFibreConnect, BizFibreConnect, ClinicConnect, ParkConnect) while being deliberately technology-agnostic — the underlying network (FWB, FTTH, or LTE/5G) is determined by coverage, not by brand. The customer simply buys "WorkConnect" and gets the best available technology at their address.

### 3.2 Speed Profile Clarification

> **⚠ IMPORTANT — SPEED PROFILE DISCLOSURE:** WorkConnect is technology-agnostic. The actual upload speed a customer receives depends on the underlying delivery technology:
>
> - **MTN Tarana G1 FWB:** Operates at a **4:1 download-to-upload ratio** (e.g. 100 Mbps down / 25 Mbps up). This is a network-level configuration applied by MTN across their entire FWB infrastructure. CircleTel, as a wholesale partner, inherits this asymmetric profile.
> - **MTN FTTH (GPON):** Genuinely **symmetrical** speeds (e.g. 100/100 Mbps).
> - **MTN 5G / LTE:** Upload speeds are **variable** and best-effort, typically 10–30% of download speed.
>
> All marketing materials, quotes, and contracts **must** state both download and upload speeds explicitly (e.g. "100/25 Mbps" for FWB, "100/100 Mbps" for FTTH). The term "symmetrical" must only be used when referring to FTTH or DFA fibre delivery.
>
> Customers requiring guaranteed symmetrical upload speeds should be redirected to **BizFibreConnect** (DFA fibre) or **HomeFibreConnect** (MTN FTTH) where available.

## 4. Technology Delivery — Coverage Determines Technology

WorkConnect is technology-agnostic by design. The customer purchases a speed tier; CircleTel selects the optimal underlying technology based on coverage at their address.

### 4.1 Technology Selection Matrix

| Priority | Technology | Infrastructure | Coverage | Upload Profile | Best For |
|----------|-----------|---------------|----------|---------------|----------|
| 1st | MTN Wholesale FTTH | DFA / MTN fibre | 37 sites, premium suburbs | **Symmetrical** (equal UL/DL) | Upload-intensive SOHO, content creators |
| 2nd | MTN Tarana G1 FWB | Fixed Wireless Broadband | 6 million homes nationally | **4:1 asymmetric** (e.g. 100/25 Mbps) | Widest coverage, good for general WFH |
| 3rd | MTN 5G Fixed Wireless | 5G NR | Metro areas | **Variable** (best-effort) | Fast deployment, good download speeds |
| 4th | MTN LTE Fixed Wireless | LTE-A | National | **Variable** (best-effort) | Fallback for areas without FWB/FTTH/5G |

### 4.2 Technology-Specific Speed Mapping

| WorkConnect Tier | FTTH Speed | FWB (Tarana) Speed | 5G Speed | LTE Speed |
|-----------------|------------|-------------------|----------|-----------|
| **Starter** | 50/50 Mbps (symmetrical) | 50/12.5 Mbps (4:1) | ~50/10 Mbps (best-effort) | ~30/8 Mbps (best-effort) |
| **Plus** | 100/100 Mbps (symmetrical)* | 100/25 Mbps (4:1) | ~100/20 Mbps (best-effort) | ~60/15 Mbps (best-effort) |
| **Pro** | 200/200 Mbps (symmetrical) | 200/50 Mbps (4:1) | ~200/40 Mbps (best-effort) | N/A — upgrade to FWB/FTTH |

*FTTH 100 Mbps not currently available as a wholesale tier — customer would receive 200 Mbps FTTH at the 100 Mbps price point (over-provision strategy).

> **Note:** Upload speeds on FWB (Tarana) are governed by MTN's network-level 4:1 DL:UL ratio. This cannot be changed per-subscriber or per-partner. Customers requiring high upload speeds (e.g. live streaming, large file uploads, media production) should be prioritised for FTTH delivery where available, or redirected to BizFibreConnect (DFA fibre).

## 10. Risk Analysis

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Cannibalisation of HomeFibreConnect** | Medium | Medium | Clear positioning: WorkConnect includes business value-adds (QoS, backup, email). Price differential is justified by features, not speed alone. |
| **Cannibalisation of SkyFibre SMB** | Low | High | WorkConnect deliberately excludes full SLA, 24/7 support, and enterprise routers. SMB remains the product for businesses needing guaranteed uptime. |
| **MTN AirFibre channel conflict (FWB)** | Low | Medium | AirFibre targets residential consumers. WorkConnect's SOHO positioning and business value-adds differentiate it from commodity residential broadband. |
| **Low margins on FTTH Starter tier** | High | Low | Prioritise FWB delivery where both technologies available. FTTH Starter margin (9.9%) is acceptable as a retention tool — churn is lower on FTTH. |
| **Router stock constraints (RG-EG105GW)** | Medium | Medium | Pre-order 200 units. Maintain RG-EW1300G as fallback for Plus tier (with QoS limitations disclosed). |
| **Market confusion between product lines** | Medium | Medium | Clear website navigation, distinct branding, and sales training. "Are you working from home?" qualifier at first touchpoint. |
