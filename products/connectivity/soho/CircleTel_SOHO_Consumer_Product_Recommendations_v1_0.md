## 1. Executive Summary

This document presents CircleTel's recommended product and service portfolio for the SOHO (Small Office/Home Office) and consumer markets in South Africa. It is structured as a phased go-to-market plan prioritising quick revenue generation with launch-ready products, while building towards comprehensive market coverage across three segments: SOHO/work-from-home professionals, suburban residential (middle-income), and township/low-income households (LSM 4–7).

The recommendations are derived from CircleTel's existing product knowledge base, including the Complete Product Portfolio v3.0, WorkConnect SOHO Portfolio v1.1, HomeFibreConnect Product Document, UmojaLink Township Portfolio, and the Telecoms 2025 industry report.

### 1.1 Strategic Context

CircleTel's current strategy (v3.0, December 2025) focuses on B2B-first execution following the MTN AirFibre channel conflict that forced the sunset of SkyFibre Residential FWA products. However, several consumer and SOHO products remain commercially viable and represent significant untapped revenue opportunities:

**HomeFibreConnect** (MTN Wholesale FTTH) is ACTIVE and unaffected by the AirFibre conflict, serving suburban residential and SOHO segments with 26–51% margins.

**WorkConnect™** is a new SOHO-specific product filling a gap no major SA ISP currently addresses, with purpose-built value-adds and 28–42% target margins.

**UmojaLink™** targets 8 million township households with prepaid connectivity from R10/day, scoring 9/10 in CircleTel's portfolio readiness assessment.

### 1.2 Revenue Opportunity Summary

| Product | Phase | Segment | Price Range | Margin | MRR Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **HomeFibreConnect** | 1 (Immediate) | Suburban | R799–R1,999/mo | 26–51% | R150k |

## 2. Phase 1 — Immediate Launch (Month 1–3)

**Phase 1 focuses exclusively on products that are already ACTIVE in the portfolio, require no additional development, and can generate revenue immediately.**

### 2.1 HomeFibreConnect — Suburban Residential

HomeFibreConnect delivers residential fibre-to-the-home via MTN's wholesale FTTH infrastructure across 37 sites in 7 regions. This product line is completely unaffected by the MTN AirFibre channel conflict and remains CircleTel's strongest consumer play.

#### Product Tiers

| Package | Speed | Retail | Wholesale | Margin | Target Customer |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **HomeFibre Starter** | 20/20 Mbps | R799/mo | R412 | 26% | Budget households |
| **HomeFibre Plus** | 50/50 Mbps | R999/mo | R542 | 38% | Average families, WFH |
| **HomeFibre Max** | 200/200 Mbps | R1,499/mo | R737 | 45% | Power users, gamers |
| **HomeFibre Ultra** | 500/500 Mbps | R1,999/mo | R837 | 51% | Premium homes |

*All speeds are symmetrical (upload = download). Prices exclude VAT.*

#### Value-Add Bundles

| Bundle | Base Product | Premium | Proposition |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Complete** | Any tier | +R199/mo | Static IP + Premium Router — business features for home |
| **Family** | Plus or higher | +R99/mo | Parental controls + QoS — multi-device optimisation |
| **Gamer** | Max or Ultra | +R149/mo | Low latency + Priority QoS — sub-5ms guarantee |
| **Work-From-Home** | Plus or higher | +R149/mo | VPN optimisation + Cloud backup — professional features |

#### Why Launch First

**Status:** ACTIVE — all four tiers are already available for sale.

**Infrastructure:** Leverages MTN's existing FTTH network (37 sites, 7 regions) with no CAPEX required.

**Margin quality:** Plus and Max tiers deliver 38–45% contribution margins; Ultra reaches 51%. Post-24-month margins improve further (36–55%) once equipment costs are recovered.

**Competitive position:** Priced competitively against Afrihost (R699–R1,497), Supersonic (R799–R2,099), and Vox (R549–R2,195) with the added advantage of Reyee WiFi 5/6 routers and Ruijie Cloud management.

**Cross-sell path:** HomeFibre customers are natural candidates for WorkConnect SOHO upgrades in Phase 2, creating an upsell pipeline.

#### Phase 1 Target

**MRR Goal: R150,000 by Month 3 (approximately 150 active subscribers at a blended ARPU of R1,000).**

## 3. Phase 2 — SOHO Launch (Month 3–6)

**Phase 2 introduces CircleTel's dedicated SOHO product, targeting a market gap that no major SA ISP currently fills with a purpose-built offering.**

### 3.1 WorkConnect™ — SOHO/WFH Professionals

WorkConnect is CircleTel's technology-agnostic SOHO connectivity product line, bridging the gap between residential broadband (HomeFibreConnect at R799–R1,999/month) and full business-grade services (SkyFibre SMB at R1,899–R6,999/month). It targets South Africa's rapidly growing segment of freelancers, remote workers, micro-businesses (1–5 people), e-commerce operators, and content creators.

#### Market Opportunity

| Metric | Estimate | Source |
|---|---|---|
| SA internet users | 50.8 million (78.9%) | ITU/Kepios 2025 |
| Remote/hybrid workers (formal sector) | ~2.5 million | Stats SA extrapolation |
| Registered freelancers (international platforms) | ~500,000 | Payoneer SA 2024 |
| SMEs in SA (0–5 employees) | ~2.6 million | SEDA Report |
| **Addressable SOHO market** | **~800,000 households** | **CircleTel estimate** |

#### Target Personas

| Persona | Description | Spend Tolerance | Key Need |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Freelancer** | Designer, copywriter, developer, VA (R8k–R30k/mo income) | R500–R1,200 | Reliable uploads, video calls |
| **The Remote Worker** | SA/international employer, WFH 3–5 days/week | R800–R1,500 | VPN stability, low latency |
| **The Micro-Business** | Sole trader or 2–5 person firm (bookkeeper, consultant) | R1,000–R2,000 | Static IP, VoIP-ready |
| **Content Creator** | YouTuber, podcaster, live-streamer, e-commerce seller | R800–R1,500 | High upload, low jitter |
| **Digital Nomad** | International worker on SA Digital Nomad Visa | R1,000–R2,000 | Fast, no contract, English support |

#### Product Tiers

| Tier | Download | Upload | Price | Margin | Value-Adds |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **WorkConnect Starter** | 50 Mbps | 12.5 Mbps* | R799/mo | 28% | VoIP QoS, extended support |
| **WorkConnect Pro** | 100 Mbps | 25 Mbps* | R1,099/mo | 35% | + Cloud backup, static IP |
| **WorkConnect Max** | 200 Mbps | 50 Mbps* | R1,499/mo | 42% | + Priority support, failover |

*\*FWB speeds are 4:1 asymmetrical (DL:UL). FTTH tiers are symmetrical. Technology is agnostic (FWB, FTTH, or LTE/5G depending on coverage).*

#### Competitive Edge

The key insight from the WorkConnect v1.1 product document is that no major SA ISP currently offers a dedicated, branded SOHO product with purpose-built value-adds. Trusc has a "Home Office/WFH" package (R439–R1,199/month), but it lacks VoIP QoS prioritisation, cloud backup integration, and extended business-hours support. Rain, Afrihost, and Vodacom all position their products as generic residential, leaving SOHO customers to self-select from consumer tiers.

WorkConnect's differentiation lies in packaging—not just speed—offering VoIP quality-of-service, cloud backup, and professional-grade support under a single SOHO brand.

#### Phase 2 Target

**MRR Goal: R350,000 by Month 12 (approximately 320 subscribers at a blended ARPU of R1,100).**

## 4. Phase 3 — Township Expansion (Month 6–12)

**Phase 3 addresses the largest volume opportunity in CircleTel's portfolio: township connectivity. This phase requires more groundwork (community installers, spaza shop distribution, brand awareness) but offers the highest scaling potential.**

### 4.1 UmojaLink™ — Township Connectivity

UmojaLink ("Unity" in Swahili) is CircleTel's strategic initiative to bridge the digital divide in South African townships. Built on MTN's Tarana G1 technology, it delivers affordable, high-speed internet to communities historically excluded from the digital economy. The product scored 9/10 in CircleTel's portfolio readiness assessment—the highest of any product line.

#### Market Opportunity

**8 million** township households in South Africa currently spend R200/GB on mobile data, with 18–28% of household income going to connectivity. UmojaLink delivers unlimited internet from R10/day, representing a **95% saving** compared to mobile data costs. This aligns with the Telecoms 2025 report's finding that five million addressable lower-income homes remain largely unconnected (only 0.4% connectivity rate), whilst fibre providers like Fibertime and Vumatel are actively investing in township infrastructure.

#### UmojaLink Home — Residential Packages

| Package | Speed | Monthly | Daily | Weekly | Target Market |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **UmojaLink Lite** | 20 Mbps | R299 | R10 | R70 | Entry users, students |
| **UmojaLink Plus** | 50 Mbps | R399 | R13 | R91 | Families, streaming |
| **UmojaLink Max** | 100 Mbps | R599 | R20 | R140 | Power users, micro-businesses |

*All packages are completely uncapped with no Fair Usage Policy. Free installation and free WiFi router included. No contracts required.*

#### UmojaLink Hotspots — Community WiFi

| Package | Duration | Price | Data | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Unity Hour** | 1 hour | R5 | 1 GB | Quick tasks, USSD banking |
| **Daily Unity Pass** | 24 hours | R10 | Unlimited | Day users, job seekers |
| **Weekly Unity Pass** | 7 days | R50 | Unlimited | Regular users |
| **Monthly Unity Pass** | 30 days | R199 | Unlimited | Heavy users, WFH |

#### Distribution & Payments

UmojaLink's distribution model is built for township realities: 5,000+ spaza shops as voucher points, with additional reach through tuck shops, salons, and community resellers earning 10% commission. Payment methods include cash vouchers, M-Pesa/eWallet/Cash Send, bank transfer, and USSD (*134*UMOJA#). WhatsApp support is available in all 11 official South African languages.

#### Social Impact Model

UmojaLink reinvests 10% of revenue into community programmes, employs 80% township residents (100+ jobs across installers, support agents, sales agents, and community managers), and delivers an estimated R50 million annual saving for communities versus mobile data costs. This aligns with government universal access mandates and creates a powerful brand story.

#### Phase 3 Target

**MRR Goal: R4,000,000 by Month 18–24 (approximately 10,000 township households at R399 average ARPU).**

## 5. Complementary Services & Add-Ons

Beyond the three core product lines, CircleTel can layer additional revenue streams across all consumer and SOHO segments.

### 5.1 Managed IT Essentials for SOHO

The Managed IT Services portfolio (v4.0) includes an Essential tier at R2,999/month targeting 1–10 user SOHO businesses with 42% margins. While primarily a B2B play, this creates a natural upsell path from WorkConnect SOHO customers who need basic IT support, Microsoft 365 management, and security services bundled with their connectivity.

### 5.2 Mobile Solutions via Arlan Partnership

The Arlan Communications partnership provides MTN Business mobile deals on a 30% commission basis without any infrastructure investment. Business Voice (R299–R999/month) and Business Data (R149–R649/month) SIMs offer an immediate cross-sell for SOHO customers needing mobile connectivity alongside their fixed-line WorkConnect service. Planned for Q1 2026 launch.

### 5.3 CloudWiFi WaaS Overlay

CloudWiFi, CircleTel's Wi-Fi-as-a-Service product, can be layered on top of any connectivity pipe to serve venues, shared workspaces, and community centres in township areas. It is a managed overlay (not a connectivity product), with CircleTel retaining hardware ownership—creating switching cost and balance sheet value.

### 5.4 AirLink FWA for Semi-Urban Estates

For suburban residential areas and business parks without fibre coverage, AirLink FWA (Reyee 60GHz and 5GHz equipment) provides a rapid-deployment alternative at R599–R1,699/month with exceptional 58–81% margins. This fills the gap between FTTH-served suburbs and township deployments.

| Provider | Product | 50 Mbps | 100 Mbps | SOHO Adds | Technology |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **CircleTel** | WorkConnect | R799 | R1,099 | ✅ Yes | FWB/FTTH/LTE |
| Trusc Wireless | Home Office/WFH | R449–R549 | R999 | Partial | Fixed Wireless |

| Provider | Speed | Price | Technology | Weather | Community |

## 8. Key Risks & Mitigations

## 9. Recommendations & Next Steps

### 9.1 Immediate Actions (Next 30 Days)

**1. Activate HomeFibreConnect consumer marketing:** Run coverage-area campaigns in the 37 MTN FTTH sites across 7 regions. Focus on HomeFibre Plus (50/50 Mbps at R999) and HomeFibre Max (200/200 Mbps at R1,499) as they deliver the best margin-to-price ratio.

**2. Finalise WorkConnect product specification:** The v1.1 document is in DRAFT status. Complete VoIP QoS testing, cloud backup partner integration, and pricing validation before Month 3 launch.

**3. Begin UmojaLink POC planning:** Identify the first two township deployment sites, recruit and train 20 community installers, and sign 10 spaza shops as voucher distributors.

**4. Establish brand clarity:** Ensure website and marketing materials clearly separate the three consumer brands (HomeFibreConnect, WorkConnect, UmojaLink) from the B2B portfolio (SkyFibre SMB, ClinicConnect, ParkConnect).

### 9.2 Strategic Priorities

**Lead with HomeFibreConnect for immediate revenue** — it is ACTIVE, requires no development, and achieves healthy margins on MTN's existing FTTH infrastructure.

**Position WorkConnect as the premium SOHO differentiator** — no competitor currently offers a dedicated SOHO brand with VoIP QoS, cloud backup, and professional support in SA.

**Treat UmojaLink as the volume growth engine** — the 8 million household addressable market, combined with prepaid flexibility and community impact, makes this CircleTel's largest long-term B2C opportunity.

**Cross-sell aggressively between segments** — HomeFibre customers upgrade to WorkConnect; WorkConnect customers add Managed IT Essential or Mobile Solutions; UmojaLink hotspots feed home broadband adoption.
