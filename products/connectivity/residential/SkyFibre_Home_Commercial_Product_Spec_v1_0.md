## 1. Executive Summary

This Commercial Product Specification (CPS) defines the SkyFibre Home portfolio — CircleTel's residential fixed wireless broadband offering targeting South African households, work-from-home professionals, and home-based businesses.

SkyFibre Home leverages MTN's Wholesale Fixed Wireless Broadband (FWB) service, utilising Tarana G1 beamforming technology operating on licensed spectrum. The service delivers **asymmetrical speeds at a 4:1 download-to-upload ratio** (e.g. 50 Mbps down / 12.5 Mbps up), consistent with the network-level configuration applied by MTN across their entire FWB infrastructure.

### 1.1 Strategic Context — The MTN AirFibre Challenge

On launch of MTN AirFibre in early 2026, MTN disrupted the residential FWA market with aggressive pricing:

| MTN AirFibre | Speed (DL/UL) | Retail Price |
|--------------|---------------|--------------|
| AirFibre 80 | 80/25 Mbps | **R499/mo** |
| AirFibre 150 | 150/50 Mbps | **R699/mo** |
| AirFibre 300 | 300/100 Mbps | **R899/mo** |

**Critical constraint:** CircleTel's wholesale cost from MTN for 50 Mbps is R499 — the same price MTN retails 80 Mbps AirFibre to consumers. This creates a fundamental margin compression that cannot be solved through price competition alone.

**Strategic response:** This document outlines a differentiation strategy focused on service quality, local support, professional installation, and value-added modules rather than price matching.

### 1.2 Strategic Metrics Summary

| Metric | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Strategic Priority | ★★ Secondary Focus | Behind SkyFibre SMB |
| Target Market | Residential households, WFH professionals | Gauteng, Western Cape, KZN metro areas |
| Speed Profile | Asymmetric 4:1 DL:UL | MTN network-level configuration |
| Base Tier Margin | 25–35% | Constrained by wholesale costs |
| Technology | MTN Tarana G1 FWA | Licensed spectrum, sub-5 ms latency |
| Coverage | 6 million homes passed | National MTN Tarana footprint |
| Pricing Model | Base + Optional Modules | Value-add differentiation |

The SkyFibre Home portfolio uses a modular architecture separating connectivity from value-added services. This allows competitive base pricing with margin recovery through service modules.

### 3.3 Tarana G1 Capacity Profiles — Technical Context

The Tarana G1 platform uses Time Division Duplexing (TDD) with a 4:1 download-to-upload ratio configured at the network level by MTN. This applies to all subscribers and cannot be changed per-customer.

| Advertised Speed | Download | Upload | Ratio |
|------------------|----------|--------|-------|
| 50 Mbps | 50 Mbps | 12.5 Mbps | 4:1 |
| 100 Mbps | 100 Mbps | 25 Mbps | 4:1 |
| 200 Mbps | 200 Mbps | 50 Mbps | 4:1 |

**Competitive implication:** CircleTel cannot differentiate on speed — MTN AirFibre uses the same technology. All positioning must focus on service quality, support, and value-added features.

## 10. Fair Usage & Acceptable Usage Policy

**SkyFibre Home is marketed as truly uncapped with no FUP.** This differentiates from MTN direct services which may apply fair usage throttling during peak periods.

### 10.1 Protected Traffic (Never Throttled)

- Video streaming (Netflix, YouTube, DSTV, Showmax)
- Video conferencing (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet)
- Online gaming
- VoIP calls (WhatsApp, Skype)
- Web browsing and email
- Cloud storage sync

### 10.2 Prohibited Activities

- Running commercial servers (web, mail, game hosting)
- Reselling or sharing connections commercially
- Cryptocurrency mining
- Network attacks, scanning, or hacking
- Continuous high-bandwidth seeding (torrents)

## 15. Strategic Options & Recommendations

### 15.1 Option A: Premium Positioning (RECOMMENDED)

**Strategy:** Accept lower volume, maintain margin through service differentiation.

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Monthly subscribers | 500–1 000 |
| Average margin | 30–35% |
| Module attach rate | 40%+ |
| Customer profile | WFH professionals, quality-seekers |

**Pros:**
- Sustainable margins
- Lower support volume
- Higher customer lifetime value
- Aligns with CircleTel brand

**Cons:**
- Limited market share
- MTN dominates volume segment

### 15.2 Option B: Aggressive Pricing with Renegotiation

**Strategy:** Negotiate better wholesale rates from MTN to enable competitive pricing.

**Required wholesale pricing:**
| Speed | Current Wholesale | Required Wholesale | For Retail |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|------------|
| 50 Mbps | R499 | R350 | R499 @ 30% margin |
| 100 Mbps | R599 | R490 | R699 @ 30% margin |
| 200 Mbps | R699 | R630 | R899 @ 30% margin |

**Negotiation leverage:**
- Unjani Clinics commitment (252 sites)
- MSC commitment compliance
- Threat to reduce residential focus

### 15.3 Option C: Exit Residential Tarana

**Strategy:** Discontinue SkyFibre Home, focus entirely on SkyFibre SMB and BizFibreConnect.

**Rationale:**
- Cannot compete with MTN direct pricing
- Low margin, high support intensity
- Residential customers more price-sensitive

**Alternative:** Refer residential leads to MTN AirFibre, earn nothing but avoid losses.

### 15.4 Recommendation

**Proceed with Option A (Premium Positioning) with Option B as parallel negotiation track.**

Focus SkyFibre Home on the WFH professional segment where service quality matters. Simultaneously pursue wholesale price renegotiation with MTN. If renegotiation succeeds, expand to broader residential market.
