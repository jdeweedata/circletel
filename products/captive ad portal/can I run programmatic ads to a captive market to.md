# can I run programmatic ads to a captive market to offer WiFi in public spaces?

Yes, absolutely — this is a well-established and growing model called **WiFi advertising via captive portals**, and it's particularly viable in South Africa. You essentially trade free internet access for ad exposure, turning your WiFi infrastructure into a revenue-generating or cost-offsetting marketing channel.

## How It Works

When a user connects to your public WiFi, they're intercepted by a **captive portal** (splash page) before gaining internet access [^1]. This is your advertising real estate. The user must either watch an ad, fill in a form, or log in via social/email to proceed — giving you both their attention and their data [^2].

South African operator **Think WiFi** already runs this model at scale, managing over 400 hotspots in townships and shopping malls where users get 30 minutes of unlimited internet after watching a brief ad [^3]. Given that nearly half of South Africa's urban residents live in townships and represent billions of rands in consumer spending, this is a highly underserved and valuable captive audience [^3].

## Ad Types You Can Run

- **Static or video ads** on the splash page before access is granted (time-based or mandatory view)
- **Sponsored access** — a third-party brand pays for the WiFi and owns the ad placement
- **Hyper-targeted ads** based on user profile data collected at login (demographics, location, behavior) [^4]
- **Redirect ads** — users are sent to a landing page, app download, or e-commerce site post-login [^2]
- **Retargeting via email/SMS** using contact data captured at sign-in [^5]


## Revenue Models

| Model | How it works | Best for |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **Ad-funded access** | Brands pay per impression/view | High-footfall venues |
| **Sponsored WiFi** | One brand sponsors the whole portal | Brand awareness campaigns |
| **Data monetization** | Sell audience insights to advertisers | Scale operators (ISPs/WISPs) |
| **Venue self-promotion** | Promote your own deals/loyalty programs | Malls, cafés, hotels |
| **Tiered access** | Free for ad viewers, paid for faster/longer | Mixed consumer segments |

## Key Platforms to Explore

Several platforms make this plug-and-play: **Linkyfi** (AVSystem), **Powerlynx**, **Wiacom**, and **Obifi** all offer captive portal management with built-in ad campaign tools, audience segmentation, and analytics [^5][^6][^7]. For the SA market specifically, **Adclick Africa** also operates a WiFi advertising network you could plug into as an inventory supplier [^2].

## What to Watch Out For

- **POPIA compliance** — collecting user data at login requires explicit consent and a clear privacy policy; this is non-negotiable in South Africa [^3]
- **Ad fatigue** — mandatory ads that are too long or too frequent will kill adoption; 15–30 second pre-roll or a single splash screen is the sweet spot
- **Hyper-targeting premium** — advertisers will pay significantly more for segmented, location-aware audiences vs. broad impressions [^4]
