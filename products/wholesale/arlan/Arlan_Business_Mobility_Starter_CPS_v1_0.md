## 1. Executive Summary

**Business Mobility Starter** is CircleTel's highest-volume Arlan/MTN reseller product. It delivers single or multi-line MTN device upgrades (smartphones, MiFi) to South African SMEs at business tariff rates, with CircleTel acting as account manager, billing intermediary, and support layer. The product requires zero capital expenditure — there is no infrastructure to deploy, no NNI port, and no installation.

### Strategic Role

Business Mobility Starter is CircleTel's cashflow engine. It is designed to generate immediate, recurring revenue from Month 1 of operations (April 2026) that directly funds Tarana FWB infrastructure deployment. The cashflow logic is explicit: 500 cumulative Arlan deals by Month 4 (July 2026) generates approximately R136,000 in cumulative commission and markup income, which covers the full cost of the first 50 Tarana FWB installations (R127,500 at R2,550 per site).

This product is sold before CircleTel has a fibre or FWB footprint in a given area. It extends CircleTel's national reach to any business on any street in South Africa that has MTN 4G or 5G coverage — which is effectively the entire formal economy.

## 2. Product Architecture

Business Mobility Starter is a single-SKU reseller arrangement. CircleTel sources deals through Arlan Communications (Pty) Ltd T/A MTN Ballito, which operates as a Master Dealer on the MTN network. CircleTel earns a share of MTN commission plus retains any markup applied above the Arlan base price.

### Recommended Deals (March 2026 — Verify Before Quoting)

The following five deals represent the optimal combinations of device appeal, price band, and margin for the R500–R999 subscription range. All are 36-month contracts on MTN's MFB (Mobile for Business) tariff family.

| Device | Plan | Subscription/mo (incl. VAT) | Term | Bundled Data | Target Segment |
|--------|------|-----------------------------|------|--------------|----------------|
| Vivo Y31 5G | MFB S+ | R455 | 36 months | 2.3GB | Entry-level staff phone, retail staff |
| Vivo V60 Lite 5G | MFB S+ | R535 | 36 months | 2.3GB | Mid-market professional, admin staff |
| Samsung Galaxy A26 5G + freebie A26 | MFB S+ | R525 | 36 months | 2.3GB | Dual-phone deal, small business owner |
| Oppo A5 Pro 4G | MFB M | R459–R509 | 36 months | 6.5GB | Field staff, delivery drivers |
| Oppo A6 Pro 5G | MFB L | R649–R759 | 36 months | 23GB | Senior staff, technical roles |

> **Note**: The Vivo Y31 5G at R455/mo falls just below the R500 target floor but is included as a loss-leader entry point when selling 2+ lines to the same customer, where the bundle economics recover. Standalone single deals at R455 should be upsold to the R535 Vivo V60 Lite or paired with a second line.

### Revenue Components Per Deal

CircleTel's income on each deal has two independent components:

**Component 1 — MTN Commission (paid monthly via Arlan by 25th of each month)**

MTN pays a commission percentage on the monthly subscription value. Arlan retains 70% and passes 30% to CircleTel. The applicable rate depends on the subscription band:

| MTN Subscription Band | MTN Commission Rate | CircleTel Share (30%) | Example Deal | CircleTel Earns/mo |
|-----------------------|--------------------|-----------------------|--------------|--------------------|
| R300–R499 | 8.75% | 2.625% | Vivo Y31 at R455 | ~R12 |
| R500–R999 | 9.75% | 2.925% | Oppo A6 Pro at R700 | ~R20 |

**Component 2 — Markup (100% retained by CircleTel)**

CircleTel may price the subscription to the customer above Arlan's base. The markup is CircleTel's gross on the difference. Minimum markup for device upgrade deals is 8% per the margin guardrails rule; target is 8–15%.

| Arlan Base Price | Markup % | CircleTel Invoice Price | Markup Revenue/mo |
|-----------------|----------|------------------------|-------------------|
| R455 | 10% | R500 | R45 |
| R535 | 10% | R589 | R54 |
| R700 | 10% | R770 | R70 |
| R759 | 12% | R850 | R91 |

**Combined revenue per deal/month**: Commission (~R12–R29) + Markup (~R40–R100) = approximately R52–R129, with an average across the recommended deal set of approximately R87/deal/month.

## 4. Wholesale Cost Structure & Margin Analysis

Business Mobility Starter has a unique cost structure: CircleTel's wholesale cost is effectively zero. There is no SIM purchase cost, no device purchase cost, and no network cost. MTN bears all infrastructure, device, and provisioning costs. CircleTel's cost of sale is limited to sales effort and support.

### Per-Deal Economics

| Deal | Arlan Base | CT Retail (10% markup) | Markup Revenue/mo | Commission/mo | Total CT Income/mo | Effective Margin % |
|------|-----------|----------------------|-------------------|---------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| Vivo Y31 5G | R455 | R501 | R45 | R12 | R57 | ~11% effective (vs zero cost) |
| Vivo V60 Lite 5G | R535 | R589 | R54 | R16 | R70 | ~12% effective |
| Samsung A26 5G x2 | R525 | R578 | R53 | R15 | R68 | ~12% effective |
| Oppo A5 Pro 4G | R484 (mid) | R532 | R49 | R14 | R63 | ~12% effective |
| Oppo A6 Pro 5G | R704 (mid) | R774 | R71 | R21 | R92 | ~12% effective |

**Margin interpretation**: Because CircleTel has no wholesale cost (zero cost of goods), the "margin %" in the traditional sense is 100% on markup revenue and 100% on commission. The figures above express commission + markup as a percentage of the Arlan base price to illustrate the yield per deal. The 25% gross margin guardrail is met on all deals because the denominator (cost) is zero.

**Sales effort cost allocation**: At an estimated 2 hours of sales effort per deal (discovery, quoting, FICA, Arlan submission), and a blended sales rate of R150/hr, the break-even is approximately R300 per deal in commission + markup over the contract lifetime. All recommended deals exceed this threshold from Month 2 onwards.

## 8. Installation & Provisioning

Business Mobility Starter has a fully remote provisioning process. There is no physical installation, no site visit, and no CircleTel technician involvement. The process is:

| Step | Action | Owner | Timeframe |
|------|--------|-------|-----------|
| 1 | Customer selects deal from CircleTel catalogue | CircleTel Sales | Day 0 |
| 2 | Customer submits FICA documents (see BRD for list) | Customer → CircleTel | Day 0–1 |
| 3 | CircleTel verifies FICA and submits order to Arlan | CircleTel → Arlan | Day 1 |
| 4 | Arlan submits to MTN for provisioning | Arlan → MTN | Day 1–2 |
| 5 | MTN activates SIM and dispatches device | MTN | Day 2–5 |
| 6 | Device delivered to customer's registered business address | MTN courier | Day 5–9 |
| 7 | CircleTel confirms activation and raises first invoice | CircleTel | Day of activation |

**FICA document set required** (see BRD Section 5 for full details):
- Certified copy of director/owner SA ID
- Proof of business registration (CIPC CoR14, CoR14.3, or equivalent)
- Proof of business address (not older than 3 months)
- Bank account confirmation letter (for debit order setup)

**No device stock held**: CircleTel does not purchase or warehouse devices. This eliminates inventory risk, working capital requirements, and logistics costs entirely.

## 12. Competitive Positioning

### The Core Advantage: Speed, Convenience, and Accountability

Business Mobility Starter is not sold on price alone. MTN's published business rates are the same through any channel. CircleTel's differentiation is operational:

| Factor | MTN Business Store | CircleTel Business Mobility Starter |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Where to buy | In-store visit required | WhatsApp / email; fully remote |
| Wait time | 1–3 hours in-store | Quote in 30 minutes; device in 3–7 days |
| Account manager | None (transactional) | CircleTel named account contact |
| Billing | MTN invoice (multiple if multi-line) | Single CircleTel invoice for all lines |
| Bundling | Mobile only | Mobile + internet from same supplier |
| Support | MTN call centre (queue) | WhatsApp direct to CircleTel support |
| Contract management | Customer manages each line independently | CircleTel tracks all contract dates and renewal windows |

**Sales pitch**: "MTN Business rates, without the MTN store queue — we handle everything."

**Upsell path**: Business Mobility Starter customers are the highest-conversion audience for WorkConnect SOHO internet. Once they trust CircleTel for mobile, offering fixed internet on the same account and invoice is a natural upsell. This makes every Business Mobility Starter customer a WorkConnect pipeline entry.

### Competitor Landscape

| Competitor | Weakness CircleTel Exploits |
|------------|---------------------------|
| MTN Business Store | Queue, no account manager, no bundled internet, no relationship |
| Vodacom Business | Different network; switching resistance can be overcome with device + plan value |
| Cell C Business | Network quality concerns; MTN coverage superiority in peri-urban areas |
| Other MTN dealers | No internet bundling; single-touch transactional; no CircleTel account management |

## 13. Risk Register

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Volume velocity below 30 deals/month | Medium | High — delays Tarana funding | Prioritise sales effort; Unjani site managers as first 20 leads; LinkedIn outreach |
| Commission payment delay from Arlan | Low | Medium — cashflow gap | Maintain 1-month operating cash buffer; track Arlan invoice dates in admin |

| April 2026 promo sheet changes pricing materially | Medium | Medium — margin compression if not updated | Mandatory price refresh before each quote; use April sheet as soon as received |
| Arlan relationship / dealer agreement changes | Low | High — channel closure | Monitor Arlan communications; retain direct MTN dealer option if volumes warrant |
| Customer FICA fails or business unregistered | Low | Low (deal aborted, no cost) | Pre-qualify on first call: "Is your business registered with CIPC?" |
| Devices out of stock at MTN | Low-Medium | Low — delay, not cancellation | Offer alternative device from same price band; do not promise delivery date before MTN confirms |
| MSC shortfall if Tarana ramp is slower than projected | Medium | Medium — monthly MSC payment | Business Mobility Starter cash directly mitigates this; accelerate Arlan volumes in months 3-4 |
