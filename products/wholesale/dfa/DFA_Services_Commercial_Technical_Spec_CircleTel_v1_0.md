## 1. Executive Summary

Dark Fibre Africa (DFA) is CircleTel's primary wholesale fibre infrastructure provider, supplying a comprehensive portfolio of dark fibre, managed connectivity, colocation, and business broadband services across South Africa. DFA is a subsidiary of Vumatel parent company Maziv and operates as an open-access fibre infrastructure provider.

This document serves as the definitive product management reference for all DFA services procured by CircleTel, detailing commercial pricing (effective 1 April 2025), technical specifications, contract terms, and strategic use cases for each service.

### Key Facts — DFA

- **Parent Company:** Maziv (Remgro-controlled CIVH)
- **Founded Network:** 2007 (fibre network deployment commenced)
- **Infrastructure:** 15 000+ km of ducting across major metros, secondary cities, and smaller towns
- **Recent Investment:** R800-million network upgrade and future-proofing (January 2025)
- **R400-million** dry underground distribution cabinet network enhancement project (launched August 2023)
- **Service Model:** Open-access wholesale provider
- **Network Availability SLA:** 99.95%

### 2.2 Network Footprint

DFA's national fibre network spans all major metropolitan areas and inter-city routes in South Africa. The network is technology-agnostic and supports any aggregation technology including Ethernet, GPON, and MPLS.

### 2.3 Pricing Principles

All DFA pricing follows a consistent structure:

- **NRC (Non-Recurring Charges):** Once-off installation, site survey, setup, and connection fees
- **MRC (Monthly Recurring Charges):** Ongoing monthly service fees
- **All prices are exclusive of VAT**
- **Annual escalation:** CPI + 2% (standard), or NERSA rate + inflation (Tachyon sites)

## 7. Infrastructure Requirements — GNNI & ENNI Ports

### 7.1 Overview

All DFA managed services (Business Broadband, Magellan, Calypte) require a network-to-network interface port at a data centre. DFA uses two port types depending on the service:

- **GNNI (Generic Network-to-Network Interface):** Used for Business Broadband services
- **ENNI (External Network-to-Network Interface):** Used for Magellan E-Access/E-Line services
- **AEP (Aggregated Ethernet Port):** Used for Calypte Metro services

### 7.2 Port Pricing Summary

| Service | Port Type | Speed | NRC | MRC |
|---------|----------|-------|-----|-----|
| Business Broadband | GNNI | 1 Gbps | R6 050 | R898 |
| Business Broadband | GNNI | 10 Gbps | R6 050 | R3 300 |
| Magellan | ENNI | 1 Gbps | R6 050 | R898 |
| Magellan | ENNI | 10 Gbps | R6 050 | R3 300 |
| Magellan | ENNI | 100 Gbps | R12 100 | R15 000 |
| Calypte Metro | AEP | 1 Gbps | R6 050 | R898 |
| Calypte Metro | AEP | 10 Gbps | R6 050 | R3 300 |
| Calypte Metro | AEP | 100 Gbps | R12 100 | R15 000 |

### 7.3 Capacity Planning

The GNNI/ENNI port is a shared resource across all subscribers on a given service. CircleTel's current infrastructure uses 1 Gbps GNNI ports at Teraco data centres (JB1 and CT1). Upgrade to 10 Gbps is recommended when subscriber counts approach 250+ on Business Broadband, or when aggregate bandwidth demand approaches port capacity.

### 9.4 Strategic Recommendations for CircleTel

1. **Term Commitments:** Always target 5-year contracts where possible for maximum NRC waiver (R0 on Helios and Magellan 1 Gbps) and 72% MRC savings.
2. **Volume Tiers:** Aggregate Helios links to move from Tier 1 to Tier 2 pricing (~5% saving).
3. **Product Mix:** Use Peregrine for core backbone, Helios for dedicated last-mile, Lumic for multi-tenant sites, and Calypte for burstable branch links.
4. **Infrastructure Sharing:** Maximise customers per GNNI port; target 100+ subscribers per 1 Gbps GNNI for optimal per-customer infrastructure cost allocation.
