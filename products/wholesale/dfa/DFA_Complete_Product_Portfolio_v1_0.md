## 1. Executive Summary

Dark Fibre Africa (DFA) provides comprehensive wholesale fibre infrastructure and managed connectivity services across South Africa. This document details all available DFA products, pricing structures, and use cases for CircleTel's internal reference.

### Key Pricing Categories

- **Non-Recurring Charges (NRC):** Once-off installation, setup, and connection fees
- **Monthly Recurring Charges (MRC):** Ongoing monthly service fees
- **Volume Discounts:** Available based on commitment levels
- **Contract Terms:** 1 to 15 years with term-based pricing

### Important Notes

- All prices **exclude VAT**
- Subject to feasibility assessment
- Annual escalations apply (typically CPI + 2%)
- Early termination penalties: Full remaining contract value

## 2. Dark Fibre Products

### 2.1 Peregrine Metro — Intra-City Dark Fibre

#### Description

Dark fibre-optic cables providing intra-city core network backhaul with unrestricted connectivity between core sites, branch sites, and aggregation nodes within metropolitan areas.

#### Key Features

- Unlimited bandwidth capacity
- No throughput restrictions
- Support for any aggregation technology (Ethernet/GPON)
- Maximum 150m float from DFA infrastructure
- Single fibre pair provision

#### Pricing Structure — Monthly Recurring Charge (per metre)

| Distance Tier | 1 Year | 2 Year | 3 Year | 4 Year | 5 Year | 10 Year | 15 Year |
|---------------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|---------|---------|
| **0–500 km** | R18.37 | R10.02 | R7.29 | R5.97 | R5.20 | R3.83 | R3.01 |
| **500–1 000 km** | R15.80 | R8.62 | R6.27 | R5.13 | R4.47 | R3.45 | R2.71 |
| **>1 000 km** | R13.04 | R7.11 | R5.18 | R4.24 | R3.69 | R2.79 | R2.54 |

#### Non-Recurring Charges

| Service | Cost |
|---------|------|
| Site survey | R15 000 per endpoint |
| Installation | R25 000 per endpoint |
| Testing & commissioning | R10 000 per link |

#### Contract Terms

- Available terms: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 10, and 15 years
- Early termination penalty: Full remaining contract value
- No subleasing permitted
- Renewals combine with initial term for better pricing

#### Use Cases

**Use Case 1: ISP Metro Backbone**
- **Scenario:** ISP connecting 5 PoPs across Johannesburg (total 25 km)
- **Solution:** 5-year Peregrine Metro contract
- **Cost:** 25 000 m × R5.20 = R130 000/month

**Use Case 2: Enterprise Data Centre Interconnect**
- **Scenario:** Bank connecting primary and disaster recovery sites (8 km)
- **Solution:** 10-year contract for maximum savings
- **Cost:** 8 000 m × R3.83 = R30 640/month
- **Benefits:** 79% cost saving vs 1-year term

### 2.2 Peregrine Long Haul — Inter-City Dark Fibre

#### Description

Dark fibre infrastructure providing unrestricted connectivity between predefined sites along long-haul routes from submarine-cable landing stations to metros.

#### Key Features

- Drop-off capabilities along routes
- Unlimited, uncapped throughput
- Strategic coverage of major routes
- Scalable at flat cost

#### Pricing Structure

Uses same tier structure as Peregrine Metro with distance-based pricing.

#### Non-Recurring Charges

| Service | Cost |
|---------|------|
| Site survey | R20 000 per endpoint |
| Installation | R35 000 per endpoint |
| Testing & commissioning | R15 000 per link |

#### Restrictions

- No equipment on specific segments (e.g., Teraco routes)
- Must connect between PoPs
- No drops on certain protected segments

#### Use Cases

**Use Case 1: National ISP Expansion**
- **Scenario:** Connecting Johannesburg to Durban (600 km)
- **Solution:** 5-year Long Haul contract
- **Cost:** 600 000 m × R4.47 = R2 682 000/month
- **Benefits:** Direct route control, unlimited capacity

**Use Case 2: Content Delivery Network**
- **Scenario:** CDN provider connecting to coastal cable landing
- **Solution:** 10-year contract from landing station to Johannesburg
- **Benefits:** Predictable costs, future-proof capacity

### 2.5 Titan — Base Station Connectivity

#### Description

Flat-rate dark fibre solution for connecting base transceiver stations in ring or star topology.

#### Key Features

- Average 5 km distance between stations
- Ring or star topology required
- Can start/end at same site
- Designed for mobile operators

#### Pricing Structure

| Configuration | Monthly Rate | NRC |
|---------------|--------------|-----|
| Single link | R12 500 | R25 000 |
| Ring topology (2 links) | R20 000 | R40 000 |
| Diverse routing | R25 000 | R50 000 |

#### Use Cases

**Use Case 1: Mobile Network Densification**
- **Scenario:** MNO adding 20 base stations in metro
- **Solution:** Titan ring topology, 5-year term
- **Benefits:** Flat-rate pricing regardless of traffic growth

**Use Case 2: Small Cell Deployment**
- **Scenario:** 5G small cell network in CBD
- **Solution:** Star topology from central aggregation point
- **Benefits:** Simplified billing, unlimited capacity

## 7. Cost Optimisation Strategies

### 7.1 Term Commitments

| Contract Term | Cost Saving vs 1-Year |
|---------------|----------------------|
| 2 Years | 45% |
| 3 Years | 60% |
| 5 Years | 72% |
| 10 Years | 79% |
| 15 Years | 84% |

### 7.2 Volume Discounts

| Annual Commitment | Discount |
|-------------------|----------|
| R100 000 – R500 000 | 5% |
| R500 000 – R1 000 000 | 10% |
| R1 000 000 – R5 000 000 | 15% |
| >R5 000 000 | Negotiable |

### 7.3 Bundle Packages

| Bundle Type | Components | Saving |
|-------------|------------|--------|
| Metro Bundle | Peregrine + Helios | 10% |
| Full Stack | Dark Fibre + Managed Services | 15% |
| Enterprise | Multiple sites (10+) | 20% |

### 7.4 Key Strategies

1. **Term Commitments:** 5-year contracts save 20–30% on monthly costs; 10-year contracts save up to 70% on dark fibre; 15-year contracts offer maximum 80% savings.

2. **Volume Discounts:** Moving from Tier 1 to Tier 2 on Helios saves approximately 5%. Aggregate services for better pricing.

3. **Product Mix:** Use Peregrine for core, Helios for last-mile. Combine Tachyon colocation with local connectivity. Mix managed services with dark fibre.

4. **Strategic Planning:** Plan network growth to maximise volume tiers. Consider longer terms for stable routes. Use burst capability instead of fixed bandwidth.
