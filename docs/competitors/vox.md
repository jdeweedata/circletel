## **1. Founding Story: The Hardware Store Owner Turned Tech CEO**

### **Douglas Reed - The Unconventional Entrepreneur**

**The Unusual Background (1975-1997)**

Douglas Reed's journey to becoming a telecom titan is perhaps the most unorthodox in South African ISP history:

- **Age 20**: Completed first year of BCom, then worked on **North Sea oil rig**
- **Age 22**: Returned to South Africa, took over **Sandton City Hardware**
- **Next 16 years**: Built career in **DIY hardware** - tools, equipment, and home improvement
- **Career roles**: Managing Director of **Mica Distributors**, Board Member of Mica Hardware Group
- **Late 30s realization**: "I was wasting time working for other people"

**The Career Pivot (1997)**

- **Age 38**: Received offer from Control Instruments to head **DataPro**, its IT support division
- **Initial status**: Small company turning over **R150,000 per month** (roughly R630,000 in 2025 money)
- **Bold move**: Took a **salary cut** and bought **30% of the business**
- **Vision**: Transform it into a national Internet service provider

**Reed's Philosophy:**
> "I took over a company that had no business plan in place. The first three years demanded **12-hour days, six days a week**."

**Why Hardware to IT Wasn't Such a Leap:**
Reed had always been interested in technology, and his hardware stores often had systems that were "ahead of their time" relative to the conservative hardware industry.

## **Conclusion: Vox's Unique Path**

Vox Telecom represents a **fundamentally different ISP success model** compared to Afrihost and WebAfrica:

**The Enterprise Formula**: While Afrihost won hearts with consumer service and WebAfrica consolidated through M&A, Vox built success on **B2B relationships, least-cost routing dominance, and vertical integration** through fibre infrastructure ownership.

**The Lesson**: You can build ISP success through **B2B focus, infrastructure ownership, and financial discipline** rather than consumer brand love - but it requires **relentless adaptation and long-term strategic vision**.

Vox's 25+ year journey proves that **multiple winning formulas exist** in telecommunications - theirs was built on enterprise relationships, vertical integration, and the ability to pivot 180 degrees when the market demanded it.
