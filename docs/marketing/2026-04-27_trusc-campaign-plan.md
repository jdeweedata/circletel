## 1. Campaign Overview

**Campaign Name:** "The 30-Day Switch Challenge"

**One-sentence summary:**  
A targeted, data-led outreach campaign converting TRUSC wireless customers in the West Coast to CircleTel's Tarana FWA or MTN LTE/5G products — using price, performance, and local service as the primary switch triggers.

**Primary Objective:**  
Convert 10% of identified TRUSC contract holders (147 of 1,470) to CircleTel within 90 days of campaign launch, generating R441,000+ in Year-1 revenue from new subscriptions and hardware margin.

**Secondary Objectives:**
- Build CircleTel brand recognition in Saldanha Bay / Vredenburg / Langebaan as the credible TRUSC alternative
- Generate 200+ qualified inbound leads from the campaign landing page
- Establish a repeatable playbook for attacking fixed wireless competitors in other markets

---

## 2. Target Audience

### Primary Segment — List A: Tarana FWA Targets (380 records)
**Who:** Business owners and office managers at SMEs in Saldanha Bay, Vredenburg, Langebaan, Paternoster, and St Helena Bay paying TRUSC R500–R2,000+/month for fixed wireless broadband.  
**Pain points:**
- Congested shared wireless during business hours (TRUSC uses unlicensed/shared spectrum)
- Billing surprises and poor support (HelloPeter rating: 2.1/5)
- Locked into old contracts with no performance guarantees
- No SLA — downtime costs them money  

**Motivation to switch:**  
Save R200–R600/month AND get dedicated licensed spectrum with a service guarantee.  
**Buying stage:** Consideration → Decision (they already know they have a connectivity problem)  
**Where they spend time:** Facebook (West Coast business groups), WhatsApp Business, local community groups, Google Search

### Secondary Segment — List B: MTN LTE/5G Targets (1,090 records)
**Who:** Businesses in TRUSC coverage area but outside Tarana range — also paying TRUSC for wireless that underperforms.  
**Pain points:** Same billing/support frustrations, but geographic constraints limit alternatives.  
**Motivation to switch:** MTN business-grade LTE/5G with guaranteed SLA, saving R50–R400/month vs current TRUSC rates.  
**Buying stage:** Awareness → Consideration (may not know MTN Business options exist at this price)

## 3. Key Messages

**Core campaign message:**  
*"You're paying TRUSC prices for TRUSC service. There's a better deal down the road."*

### Supporting Messages by Pain Point

| Pain Point | Message | Proof Point |
|---|---|---|
| Congestion / slow speeds | "Dedicated licensed spectrum — no sharing, no peak-hour slowdown" | Tarana W-60 uses licensed mmWave; TRUSC uses unlicensed shared spectrum |
| Poor support | "Local CircleTel team, same-day response" | CircleTel is West Coast-based; TRUSC HelloPeter 2.1/5 |
| High cost | "Same or better speed, 30–40% less per month" | Tier-for-tier price comparison table (R449 vs R799 for 10Mbps uncapped) |
| No SLA | "SLA-backed connectivity — we put it in writing" | CircleTel/MTN Business SLA included in contract |
| Lock-in anxiety | "No long lock-in. Switch in 30 days." | 24-month MTN contracts start from R99/mo with zero pay-in on UYO |

### Message Variations by Channel
- **WhatsApp / Direct outreach:** Personal, conversational. "Hi [First Name], I noticed you're with TRUSC in Saldanha — we can save you R[X]/month on the same speeds."
- **Facebook / Social ads:** Punchy contrast. "TRUSC charges R799/mo for 10Mbps. We charge R449. Same speed. Better reliability."
- **Landing page / SEO:** Problem-aware. "Switching from TRUSC in the West Coast? Here's what CircleTel customers get instead."
- **Email:** Data-led. Subject: "Your current TRUSC package vs what CircleTel offers: a quick comparison."

## 4. Channel Strategy

### Channel 1: Direct WhatsApp Outreach (HIGH PRIORITY)
**Why:** We have physical addresses for 1,470 TRUSC customers. Many West Coast SMEs are WhatsApp-first. Direct, personal, and zero ad spend.  
**Format:** Personalised message sequence (3 touches over 14 days) — introduction → comparison → offer expiry  
**Effort:** High (requires manual sending or WA Business API integration)  
**Budget:** R0 (organic) or R2,000–R5,000/mo for WA Business API

### Channel 2: Campaign Landing Page — circletel.co.za/switch-from-trusc (HIGH PRIORITY)
**Why:** Captures organic search traffic ("TRUSC alternative West Coast"), gives sales team a link to send, and drives inbound leads.  
**Format:** Single CTA page — savings calculator, comparison table, testimonials, "Get a quote" form  
**Effort:** Medium (page build ~2 days)  
**Budget:** R0 (owned)

### Channel 3: Facebook/Instagram Paid Ads — West Coast Geo-Targeted (MEDIUM)
**Why:** TRUSC customers are on Facebook; West Coast communities are very Facebook-active. Geo-target Saldanha Bay, Vredenburg, Langebaan, Paternoster.  
**Format:** Carousel comparison ads + video testimonial (30s)  
**Effort:** Medium  
**Budget:** R3,000–R8,000/mo for meaningful reach in these micro-markets

### Channel 4: Google Search Ads (MEDIUM)
**Why:** Capture high-intent searchers: "TRUSC cancel contract", "ISP Saldanha Bay", "business internet West Coast"  
**Format:** Responsive search ads → landing page  
**Effort:** Low (once set up)  
**Budget:** R2,000–R4,000/mo (low competition keywords in this geography)

### Channel 5: Local Community Groups / Nextdoor-equivalent (LOW-MEDIUM)
**Why:** West Coast towns have active Facebook community groups where ISP complaints regularly surface. Authentic, high-trust channel.  
**Format:** Community manager posts helpful content; responds to TRUSC complaint threads  
**Effort:** Medium (requires monitoring + authentic engagement)  
**Budget:** R0

### Channel 6: Email Sequence to Geocoded Businesses (MEDIUM)
**Why:** 833+ geocoded business addresses — with email enrichment, can run targeted drip sequence.  
**Format:** 4-email sequence over 21 days (see email sequence brief)  
**Effort:** Medium  
**Budget:** R500–R1,500/mo (email platform)
