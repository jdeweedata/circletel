## 5. SALES GAP — THE MOST CRITICAL CONSTRAINT

### 5.1 The Problem

CircleTel has zero dedicated sales capacity. The MD is the only person originating, qualifying, and closing deals. At an estimated 40% of his time on sales (~16 hours/week), and assuming a conservative 20 hours per deal cycle (from lead to signed contract for SkyFibre SMB), the MD can close approximately 3–4 new customers per month.

The Bootstrap Execution Plan targets 45 active customers by Week 13 (Month 3). At 3–4 closings/month, the MD can deliver 9–12 customers in that period — a 73–80% shortfall against target.

Meanwhile, TK's Facebook campaigns generated 30 leads in the analysis period, of which 18 (60%) received no follow-up. The leads exist. The sales capacity to process them does not.

### 5.2 The Sales Funnel is Broken at Three Points

| Stage | What Happens | Who Owns It | Problem |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Lead Generation | Facebook/LinkedIn ads drive enquiries to WhatsApp | TK | Ads target areas where product is not available (67% out of coverage) |
| Lead Response & Qualification | WhatsApp enquiry comes in via ZOHO Desk | Tamsyn (50% time) | 60% of leads get no follow-up. Tamsyn is buried in support tickets. No SLA for lead response time. |
| Coverage Check & Quoting | Check MTN Tarana/DFA coverage, generate quote | MD / Tamsyn | No systematic coverage pre-qualification before campaign launch. Manual process. |
| Closing & Onboarding | Contract, FICA/RICA, provisioning | MD + Tamsyn | MD does the closing. Tamsyn does onboarding. Both are part-time on these tasks. |
| Installation & Activation | Schedule technician, complete install | Jarryd (50% time) | Dependent on contractor availability. Jarryd also manages stores. |

### 5.3 What It Takes to Close the Sales Gap — Bootstrap-Compatible Options

Given the cash position (R86K, burning R198K/month), a full-time Sales Manager at R50K CTC is unaffordable. The options that fit the bootstrap constraints:

**Option A — Commission-Only Sales Agent (Recommended as immediate action)**

| Parameter | Detail |
|:---|:---|
| Structure | Independent contractor, 100% commission, no base |
| Commission | 15–20% of first 3 months' MRR per signed customer |
| Example payout | SkyFibre SMB at R2,200 ARPU × 15% × 3 months = R990 per sign-up |
| Target | 10 new customers/month |
| Monthly commission cost at target | ~R9,900 |
| Cost only if revenue is generated | Yes — zero risk to cash flow |
| Where to find | Gumtree, LinkedIn, existing IT freelancer network, Arlan Communications channel |

**Option B — Tamsyn time reallocation + lead response SLA**

| Parameter | Detail |
|:---|:---|
| Structure | Increase Tamsyn's CircleTel allocation from 50% to 75% (if NewGen allows) |
| Additional cost | ~R6,400/month (25% of R25.74K CTC) |
| Focus shift | Dedicate mornings (08:00–12:00) exclusively to lead response and onboarding. Afternoons for support tickets. |
| Expected impact | Reduce lead response time from days/never to <4 hours. Recover ~10 of the 18 leads currently dying per campaign cycle. |
| Prerequisite | Define a lead response SLA: all new WhatsApp enquiries responded to within 2 hours during business hours. |

**Option C — Part-time telesales (Month 4+ when MRR supports it)**

| Parameter | Detail |
|:---|:---|
| Structure | Part-time employee, 4 hours/day, morning shift |
| Base salary | R8,000–R10,000/month |
| Commission | 5% of MRR for first 3 months per customer |
| Focus | Cold-call office parks in Tarana coverage areas, follow up on all inbound leads, book meetings for MD |
| Trigger | Only hire when MRR exceeds R80K/month (covers the cost with margin) |
| Expected impact | 8–12 additional qualified meetings/month for MD to close |
