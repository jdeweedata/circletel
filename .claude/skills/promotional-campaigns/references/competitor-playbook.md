## WiruLink (RUSH Network)

### Profile
- **Est.:** 2010 (16 years established)
- **Technology:** Proprietary FWA (RUSH Network open-access)
- **Coverage:** **12 million people, 4 million homes, 1.2 million businesses** (primarily Gauteng + Bloemfontein, Northern Cape expansion)
- **Pricing:** R199-R928/mo (from R199/10Mbps on RUSH)
- **Installation:** R7,500+ equipment
- **Latency:** Sub-15ms claimed
- **Strengths:** Massive coverage footprint, established ISP partnerships on RUSH
- **Weaknesses:** 2010-era technology foundation, English-only support, equipment cost barrier

### Common WiruLink Campaigns
1. Price locks (e.g., "R799 forever")
2. Installation deals
3. Speed upgrade promotions

### CircleTel Response Strategy

**Default Strategy:** DIFFERENTIATE on technology

**Why Not Price Match:**
WiruLink's RUSH network uses 2010-era technology. Our Tarana G1 delivers 10× better latency. Don't compete on price — compete on performance.

**Counter-Messaging:**
| Their Claim | Our Response |
|-------------|--------------|
| "Established 14 years" | "Built with 2026 technology, not 2010" |
| "Unlimited speeds" | "Sub-5ms latency. Because speed means nothing if video calls buffer." |
| "R799/mo" | "Your business is worth more than the cheapest option" |

**Recommended Campaign:**
```
Name: "The Latency Challenge"
Offer: Free 30-day trial with speed/latency comparison
Message: "Feel the difference. Sub-5ms vs. legacy FWA."
Target: WiruLink customers reporting congestion issues
CTA: "Book your free Tarana trial"
```

**Sales Talking Points:**
1. "WiruLink's network was designed when Zoom didn't exist"
2. "Ask them their average latency — we guarantee <5ms"
3. "Their technology can't support multiple video calls per business"
4. "No multilingual support — we serve all South Africans"

## Rain

### Profile
- **Technology:** Africa's first standalone 5G network (exclusively wireless, no fibre)
- **Coverage:** 3,000+ own towers + Vodacom/MTN roaming (5G: Gauteng, CPT; 4G: national)
- **Consumer:** RainOne Home R649-R1,049/mo (free 5G router + 2 mobile SIMs)
- **Business:** rainOne Work from R795/mo (30Mbps) - **NO SLA, WhatsApp-only support**
- **Strengths:** Low price, lifestyle brand, free router (the101 Wi-Fi 6/7)
- **Weaknesses:** Network consistency issues, WhatsApp-only support, no SLAs, no PBX/VoIP

### Common Rain Campaigns
1. "Unlimited" 5G at aggressive prices
2. No contract offers
3. Student/young professional targeting

### CircleTel Response Strategy

**Default Strategy:** IGNORE for consumer segment, DIFFERENTIATE for business

**Key Insight:**
Rain targets price-sensitive consumers. CircleTel targets businesses needing reliability. Different markets. Don't react to every Rain campaign.

**When to Respond:**
Only if Rain specifically targets SME/business segment with dedicated business offerings.

**Counter-Messaging:**
| Their Claim | Our Response |
|-------------|--------------|
| "Unlimited 5G" | "Unlimited congestion at peak hours" |
| "R399/mo" | "What's downtime costing your business?" |
| "No contract" | "No SLA. No guarantees. No business support." |

**Recommended Campaign (if needed):**
```
Name: "Business-Grade, Not Best-Effort"
Offer: SkyFibre SMB with 30-day money-back guarantee
Message: "Your cloud apps. Your video calls. Your reputation.
         Don't trust them to a network designed for Netflix."
Target: SMEs who tried Rain for business and experienced issues
CTA: "Upgrade to dedicated connectivity"
```

**Sales Talking Points:**
1. "Rain's 'unlimited' has 50GB/day fair use caps"
2. "5G towers are shared with millions of consumers"
3. "No business SLA — you're the same priority as someone streaming TikTok"
4. "We install dedicated equipment just for your business"

## MTN

### Profile
- **Subscribers:** 39.2 million SA mobile
- **Speed:** Fastest avg mobile download (**82.48 Mbps**)
- **Technology:** Full-stack (FTTH, LTE, 5G, FWA via Tarana, AirFibre)
- **AirFibre FWA:** **3.4 million+ households** (largest FWA footprint in SA)
- **Pricing:** Aggressive - Shesha 5G from **R330/mo**, fibre 50% price cuts
- **Strengths:** Network speed leadership, month-to-month flexibility, massive AirFibre coverage
- **Weaknesses:** Supersonic brand has poor CX ratings, complex enterprise processes

### Special Consideration

**MTN is our wholesale partner for Tarana FWA.** We can't directly attack their technology. Instead, differentiate on service experience.

| "SiYa AI chatbot" | "AI that speaks all 11 official languages" |

**Recommended Campaign:**

```
Message: "The connectivity you trust. The service you deserve.
         Enterprise-grade without enterprise bureaucracy."
Target: MTN business customers frustrated with service
CTA: "Get a quote in 5 minutes (not 5 meetings)"
```

**Sales Talking Points:**
1. "We use MTN's Tarana infrastructure — same technology, better service"
2. "Get a human on the phone in under 60 seconds"
3. "No corporate bureaucracy — decisions in hours, not weeks"
4. "Transparent pricing online — no sales meetings required"

## Vodacom

### Profile
- **Subscribers:** 50.7 million mobile (largest in SA)
- **Technology:** Full-stack, market leader (FTTH, 5G ~52% population coverage, SD-WAN, IoT)
- **Capex:** R12 billion annual, R60 billion 5-year investment plan
- **Consumer Fibre:** From ~R499/mo (20Mbps) on 7+ FNOs + self-build
- **Business:** Bespoke enterprise pricing, Cisco Meraki SD-WAN (47 African countries)
- **Price increases:** 4.5-9% annually (premium positioning)
- **Maziv Deal:** Approved August 2025 — now has co-control of SA's largest fibre infrastructure
- **Strengths:** Brand dominance, ecosystem bundling (gaming, mesh, battery routers), 5G coverage
- **Weaknesses:** Premium pricing, enterprise focus leaves SME underserved

### CircleTel Response Strategy

**Default Strategy:** FLANK — target their underserved SME segment

**Key Insight:**
Vodacom focuses on enterprise and consumer. SMEs (2-50 staff) are underserved — too small for enterprise team, too business-critical for consumer products.

**Counter-Messaging:**
| Their Claim | Our Response |
|-------------|--------------|
| "Market leader" | "Leaders in complexity. We're leaders in simplicity." |
| "Enterprise-grade" | "You don't need enterprise complexity. You need it to work." |
| "Premium service" | "Premium results. Not premium prices." |

**Recommended Campaign:**
```
Name: "Enterprise Tech, SME Service"
Offer: SkyFibre SMB with dedicated account manager
Message: "Get the connectivity multinationals use.
         Without the multinational pricing or bureaucracy."
Target: Growing SMEs (10-50 staff) currently on Vodacom
CTA: "See what enterprise connectivity costs at SME scale"
```

## Quick Response Decision Tree

```
When competitor launches campaign:

1. Is it in our target segment?
   └── NO → IGNORE
   └── YES → Continue

2. Are they attacking our technology?
   └── NO → Value-add response
   └── YES → Technology differentiation campaign

3. Is it price-focused?
   └── YES → Lead with value, not price
   └── NO → Match positioning, add CircleTel advantage

4. Is it a strategic account we must protect?
   └── YES → Consider matching (MD approval)
   └── NO → Stick to differentiation strategy
```
