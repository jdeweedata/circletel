## Market Trends (2024-2025)

### Price Trajectory
- Consumer prices rising after years of decline
- Telkom: 12% increase (steepest)
- Vumatel, Zoom Fibre, Herotel: All increased
- Vodacom: 4.5-9% annual increases

### Growth Segments

| Segment | Penetration | Opportunity |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Affluent homes | 48.7% connected | Saturated |
| Middle-income homes | 16.5% connected | Large growth |
| Lower-income homes | 0.4% connected | Frontier |

### Technology Shifts
- 5G FWA becoming fibre competitor (46.64% population coverage)
- MTN AirFibre: 3.4M households covered
- Rain: First standalone 5G network in Africa

## Pricing Decision Framework

### Margin Targets by Segment

| Segment | Target Margin | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| Consumer FTTH | 25-35% | Competitive pressure |
| SOHO | 30-40% | WorkConnect tier |
| SMB | 35-50% | SkyFibre sweet spot |
| Enterprise | 40-55% | Value-add services |
| WaaS | 45-60% | Hardware + recurring |

### Price Positioning Rules

1. **Consumer**: 5-10% below Afrihost/Cool Ideas on comparable FNOs
2. **Business**: Above Telkom Easy Connect, below Vox Multi-tenant
3. **Enterprise**: Quote-based with transparent SLAs
4. **WaaS**: Premium positioning with full managed service

### Red Lines

- Never below R1,299 for SkyFibre SMB (margin floor)
- Never above Vox Premium for equivalent business service
- Always include static IP (key differentiator)
- Always offer month-to-month (flexibility differentiator)
