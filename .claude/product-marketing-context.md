# CircleTel Product Marketing Context

**Company Stage**: Startup (growth marketing focus)
**Market**: South African ISP (B2B + B2C)
**Primary Goal**: Customer conversion through outcome-focused messaging

---

## Brand Voice

### Tone
- **Confident but not arrogant** — We know our stuff, but we're approachable
- **Empathetic** — We understand the frustration of unreliable internet
- **Direct** — Get to the point, no corporate fluff
- **South African** — Use local context, understand load-shedding, data costs, township realities

### What We Sound Like
- "Your connection should work as hard as you do"
- "Internet that keeps up with your life"
- "No contracts. No surprises. Just fast internet."

### What We DON'T Sound Like
- Clickbait: "You won't BELIEVE these speeds!"
- Hype: "Revolutionary game-changing connectivity solution"
- Corporate: "Leveraging synergies to optimize your digital experience"
- Desperate: "LIMITED TIME! ACT NOW!"

---

## Growth Marketing Principles

### 1. Lead with Outcomes, Not Features
People don't buy internet — they buy what internet enables.

| Feature | Outcome |
|---------|---------|
| 100Mbps download | "Stream 4K on every screen without buffering" |
| 99.9% uptime | "Never miss that important Teams call" |
| Low latency | "React faster than your opponents in-game" |
| Uncapped data | "Work, stream, and download without watching your usage" |
| Quick installation | "Online in 7 days, not 7 weeks" |

### 2. Specificity Beats Vagueness
- **Vague**: "Fast, reliable internet"
- **Specific**: "50Mbps fibre with 99.9% uptime — enough for 4 people streaming HD"

### 3. Address the Real Fear
South Africans have been burned by ISPs:
- Hidden costs and surprise fees
- 24-month contracts with no exit
- "Up to" speeds that are never achieved
- Installation delays stretching weeks
- Support that never answers

**Counter each fear explicitly** in copy.

### 4. Social Proof > Self-Promotion
- Customer testimonials with real names and areas
- "4,500+ households connected in Gauteng"
- Google/Hellopeter ratings with actual numbers

## Customer Personas & Outcome Messaging

### 1. Remote Worker / WFH Professional
**Who**: Corporate employee or contractor working from home
**Pain**: Dropped calls, slow uploads, unreliable connection during important meetings
**Fear**: Looking unprofessional, missing deadlines, losing clients
**Outcome**: "Close that deal with crystal-clear Teams calls"

**Copy angles**:
- "Never say 'sorry, my internet dropped' again"
- "Work-from-home internet that works as hard as you do"
- "Stable enough for back-to-back Zoom calls"

---

### 2. Media Professional / Content Creator
**Who**: Video editor, YouTuber, graphic designer, photographer
**Pain**: Hours waiting for uploads, cloud syncing that never finishes, interrupted renders
**Fear**: Missing deadlines, losing clients to faster competitors
**Outcome**: "Upload that 4K video in minutes, not hours"

**Copy angles**:
- "Symmetric speeds for creators who can't wait"
- "Your Dropbox syncs before your coffee's cold"
- "Upload speeds that match your creativity"

---

### 3. Homeschooling Parent
**Who**: Parent with kids learning online (full homeschool or hybrid)
**Pain**: Kids can't join classes, constant buffering, connection drops during exams
**Fear**: Kids falling behind academically
**Outcome**: "Reliable learning for every child, every day"

**Copy angles**:
- "Never miss another online lesson"
- "Stable enough for exam submissions, streaming lessons, and homework research — all at once"
- "Education-grade internet for home classrooms"

---

### 4. Gamer
**Who**: Competitive or casual gamer, often younger demographic
**Pain**: High ping, lag spikes, slow game downloads, disconnections during ranked matches
**Fear**: Losing rank, ruining team games, waiting days to download new releases
**Outcome**: "Low ping for clutch plays"

**Copy angles**:
- "Single-digit ping. Zero excuses."
- "Download Warzone tonight, not next weekend"
- "Fibre that doesn't cost you rank"

---

### 5. Business Owner / Entrepreneur
**Who**: Small business owner, office manager, SMME decision-maker
**Pain**: Staff complaining, VoIP dropping, POS systems failing, productivity lost
**Fear**: Lost revenue, angry customers, looking unprofessional
**Outcome**: "Run your business without worrying about connectivity"

**Copy angles**:
- "Your POS system works. Every transaction. Every time."
- "Staff video calls without the 'can you hear me now?'"
- "Business internet with business support"

---

### 6. Township Job Seeker / Student
**Who**: Young person looking for work, studying remotely, needs affordable connectivity
**Pain**: Can't afford data, misses job applications, can't attend online interviews
**Fear**: Missing opportunities, staying unemployed, falling behind
**Outcome**: "Stay connected to opportunities"

**Copy angles**:
- "Affordable data to find your next opportunity"
- "Apply for jobs without running out of data"
- "Student-friendly pricing, professional-grade connection"

**Special note**: This persona needs special pricing (sponsored data, community hotspots, student plans). Messaging should be dignified, not pitying.

---

### 7. Household Decision-Maker
**Who**: Family member responsible for utilities, often the one who pays bills
**Pain**: Family complaining, multiple devices fighting for bandwidth, unexplained charges
**Fear**: Overpaying, being locked into bad contracts, family frustration
**Outcome**: "One connection for the whole family"

**Copy angles**:
- "Everyone streams. Nobody buffers."
- "No contracts. No surprises on your bill."
- "Enough bandwidth for the whole house"

## Competitor Positioning

### What SA ISPs Get Wrong
1. **Contract lock-in** — We're month-to-month
2. **"Up to" speeds** — We quote real speeds
3. **Hidden installation fees** — We're transparent upfront
4. **Faceless support** — We answer the phone
5. **One-size-fits-all** — We have plans for every situation

### How We're Different
- **No contracts** (month-to-month flexibility)
- **Free installation** (no surprise fees)
- **Real human support** (local, responsive)
- **Transparent pricing** (what you see is what you pay)
- **Quick activation** (online in 7 days, not 7 weeks)

---

## CTA Language

### Avoid
- "Sign Up" (generic)
- "Get Started" (vague)
- "Submit" (bureaucratic)
- "Click Here" (dated)

### Use
- "Check My Coverage" (specific, low commitment)
- "See Plans for My Area" (personalized)
- "Get Connected" (outcome-focused)
- "Compare Plans" (helpful, not salesy)
- "Chat With Us" (personal, accessible)

## Writing Checklist

Before publishing any copy:

- [ ] Is the headline outcome-focused, not feature-focused?
- [ ] Would a real customer say this describes their problem?
- [ ] Have we addressed at least one fear/objection?
- [ ] Is the CTA specific and action-oriented?
- [ ] Does it sound like a human wrote it?
- [ ] No clickbait, hype, or corporate jargon?
- [ ] Does it respect all personas (including township/student)?
- [ ] Is the pricing/promise accurate and deliverable?
